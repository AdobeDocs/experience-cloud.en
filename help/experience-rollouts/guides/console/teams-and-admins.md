# Teams and their admins {#teams-and-admins}

In Experience Rollouts, a **team** is the organizational unit that owns applications and manages feature flags. All access, permissions, and feature flag configuration are scoped to a team.

## Team structure {#team-structure}

Teams in Experience Rollouts have a flat structure — all members with the same role share the same permissions within the team. There is no hierarchy within a role. Different roles carry different levels of access. See [User roles](../teams/user-roles.md) for a full breakdown.

A team can own multiple applications. All feature flags, feature groups, and releases for those applications are managed within the team context.

## Team admins {#team-admins}

Each team has one or more **admins**. Admins are responsible for:

* Adding and removing team members
* Assigning and changing user roles
* Approving or rejecting access requests from new members
* Managing applications owned by the team

>[!TIP]
>
>If you are unsure who the admin for your team is, check with your product or engineering lead, or reach out to your organization's Experience Rollouts contact.

## Finding your team {#finding-your-team}

Before requesting access, identify whether a team already exists for your project:

1. Check with your product or engineering lead.
2. Review the teams visible in the Experience Rollouts console if you already have limited access.
3. Ask your organization's Experience Rollouts admin for a list of active teams.

If no team exists for your project, you need to create one. See [Create a new team](create-a-new-team.md).
